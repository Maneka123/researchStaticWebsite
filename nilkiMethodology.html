Methodology

The methodology for predicting cat diseases using machine learning and artificial intelligence, combined with integrating Google Maps for practical applications, involved a comprehensive approach. Initially, we collected and prepared the data, which included gathering symptom data of cats from a comprehensive veterinary dataset. This data was meticulously cleaned to handle missing values, remove duplicates, and ensure consistency. Feature engineering was performed to convert categorical data into a numerical format and to select relevant features for the prediction models.For the algorithm selection, multiple machine learning algorithms were evaluated to identify the most suitable model for disease prediction. The study focused on two primary algorithms: Decision Tree Classifier and Random Forest Classifier. The training process involved splitting the data into training and testing sets, which were used to build and validate the models. Hyperparameters were tuned to optimize performance, and cross-validation techniques were implemented to ensure the robustness and generalizability of the models.The system design included developing an architecture to integrate the trained models into a user-friendly application capable of handling real-time input of cat symptoms and providing accurate disease predictions.A significant aspect of the methodology was the integration of Google Maps. This was utilized to find veterinarians and animal clinics within a 2km distance of the user and to provide directions.This integration ensured users could quickly locate and reach the nearest veterinary care.By systematically collecting and preparing data, selecting appropriate algorithms, training and validating models, designing an efficient system, and integrating Google Maps for practical applications, the methodology aimed to develop a reliable tool for predicting cat diseases. The use of advanced machine learning techniques, particularly the Random Forest Classifier, underscored the potential for accurate and timely veterinary diagnostics, while the Google Maps integration ensured practical accessibility to veterinary services.
