Literature Survey

The literature survey reveals significant advancements in utilizing deep learning technologies, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs), across various domains related to pet care and animal welfare. These advancements include the development of feline image analytics systems capable of predicting key attributes like breed, age, gender, stamina, and emotion, as well as robust age and gender classification of unfiltered real-world faces. Furthermore, deep learning frameworks have been effectively employed for accurate classification of dog and cat breeds using images, while novel approaches, such as pet sentiment analysis and facial expression recognition in animals, demonstrate substantial improvements in accuracy compared to traditional methods. Challenges in dermatology diagnosis have been addressed with the introduction of deep convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) for skin disease detection, and innovative methodologies combining CNNs and vision transformers have been proposed for precise classification of a dog's age based on facial images. Overall, this comprehensive literature review underscores the significance of deep learning techniques in advancing the understanding and application of computer vision in pet care and animal welfare, while also highlighting areas for further research and development.
